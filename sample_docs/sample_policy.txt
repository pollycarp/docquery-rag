ACME CORPORATION — EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK
Version 2.4 | Effective Date: January 1, 2025

================================================================================
SECTION 1: INTRODUCTION
================================================================================

Welcome to ACME Corporation. This handbook outlines the policies, procedures,
and expectations that govern employment at ACME. All employees are required to
read, understand, and comply with the contents of this handbook.

This handbook is not a contract of employment. ACME reserves the right to
modify, revoke, or add policies at any time.

================================================================================
SECTION 2: CODE OF CONDUCT
================================================================================

2.1 General Conduct
Employees are expected to behave professionally and ethically in all
interactions, whether with colleagues, clients, or the public. Conduct that
disrupts the workplace or brings ACME into disrepute is grounds for
disciplinary action up to and including termination.

2.2 Conflict of Interest
Employees must disclose any outside employment or personal relationships that
could conflict with their duties at ACME. Failure to disclose a conflict of
interest may result in immediate dismissal.

2.3 Confidentiality
All business information, including customer data, financial records, and
trade secrets, must be kept strictly confidential. Employees must sign a
Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) on their first day of employment.

================================================================================
SECTION 3: LEAVE POLICY
================================================================================

3.1 Annual Leave
Full-time employees are entitled to 20 days of paid annual leave per calendar
year. Leave must be approved by the employee's line manager at least 2 weeks
in advance.

3.2 Sick Leave
Employees are entitled to 10 days of paid sick leave per year. A medical
certificate is required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days.

3.3 Parental Leave
Primary caregivers are entitled to 16 weeks of paid parental leave.
Secondary caregivers are entitled to 4 weeks of paid parental leave.
Parental leave must be taken within 12 months of the child's birth or adoption.

3.4 Unpaid Leave
Requests for unpaid leave beyond standard entitlements will be considered
on a case-by-case basis and require approval from HR and the department head.

================================================================================
SECTION 4: REMOTE WORK POLICY
================================================================================

4.1 Eligibility
Employees who have completed their probation period (90 days) are eligible
to apply for remote work arrangements. Eligibility is subject to role
requirements and manager approval.

4.2 Equipment
ACME will provide a laptop and necessary software for approved remote workers.
Employees are responsible for maintaining a safe and productive home workspace.
ACME is not liable for personal equipment used for work purposes.

4.3 Data Security
Remote workers must connect to the ACME VPN when accessing internal systems.
Storing company data on personal devices or unauthorized cloud services is
strictly prohibited.

================================================================================
SECTION 5: PERFORMANCE & REVIEWS
================================================================================

5.1 Performance Review Cycle
All employees undergo a formal performance review twice per year:
  - Mid-year review: July
  - Annual review: December

5.2 Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)
Employees who consistently fail to meet performance expectations may be placed
on a Performance Improvement Plan. A PIP outlines specific targets and a
timeframe (typically 30–90 days) within which improvement is expected.

5.3 Promotions
Promotion decisions are based on performance review scores, tenure, and
available positions. Employees must have a minimum rating of "Meets Expectations"
in their most recent review to be considered for promotion.

================================================================================
SECTION 6: DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURE
================================================================================

6.1 Verbal Warning
The first step in the disciplinary process for minor infractions is a
verbal warning documented in the employee's file.

6.2 Written Warning
A written warning is issued for repeated minor infractions or a single
serious infraction. The written warning details the issue and expectations
going forward.

6.3 Final Written Warning
If the behaviour continues after a written warning, a final written warning
is issued. At this stage, the employee is advised that further infractions
may result in dismissal.

6.4 Dismissal
Dismissal may occur after a final written warning or immediately for gross
misconduct (e.g., fraud, violence, serious breach of confidentiality).

================================================================================
SECTION 7: GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE
================================================================================

Employees who have a grievance should first attempt to resolve it informally
with their line manager. If this is not possible or appropriate, a formal
grievance can be submitted to HR in writing.

HR will acknowledge the grievance within 5 business days and schedule a
hearing within 15 business days. Employees may be accompanied by a colleague
or union representative at the hearing.
